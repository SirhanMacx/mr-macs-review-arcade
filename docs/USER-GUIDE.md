# Mr. Mac's Review Arcade — Student Guide

A friendly walkthrough of every screen, button, and secret in the arcade.
Made for the students who actually have to use it. No teacher jargon, no
fluff — just everything you need to know to grind shards, climb the
leaderboard, and crush your next exam.

> **Live site:** https://sirhanmacx.github.io/mr-macs-review-arcade

---

## 1. Welcome

Mr. Mac's Review Arcade is **199+ review games in one website**. AP
Psychology, AP World, AP Euro, AP Human Geo, AP Macro, AP Micro, AP
Government, APUSH, Global History 9 + 10, U.S. History, full Regents
practice exams, and middle-school review for Grades 5–8. Every game is a
real arcade game — Brick Breaker, Snake, Match-3, Tower Defense, Pinball,
Frogger, Whack-a-Mole — re-skinned around the actual content from your
exams.

**You don't need an account.** No login, no email, no password. Everything
you do — your name, your avatar, your shards, your achievements, your
streak — lives only in your browser. Close the tab, come back tomorrow,
it's all still there. Switch browsers? Different machine? You'll start
fresh.

The whole arcade runs offline once it loads. School Wi-Fi flaky? Doesn't
matter. The shards keep counting.

> **Try this!** Open the live site in a fresh tab right now. The first
> thing you'll see is the lobby with the rotating featured cabinet — pick
> any one of those, and you're playing in under five seconds.

## 2. Getting Started

The first time you load the arcade, an onboarding card pops up. Three
quick decisions and you're done.

### Step 1 — Pick a name

Type whatever you want. Real name, gamertag, your dog's name. It only
shows on the local leaderboard so don't sweat it. You can change it later
in the Profile Drawer.

### Step 2 — Pick an avatar

You get 24 emoji to choose from. The mascot, the wizard, the alien, the
pizza slice — they're all valid. (Power user move: you can paste a custom
image URL in the Profile Drawer if you really want to.)

### Step 3 — Pick a course

This is the only one that actually matters for the experience. Pick the
course you're studying — AP Psych, Global 10, etc. — and the arcade
filters everything to that course by default. The hub still lets you
play other courses from the menu, but your "Today's Plan" recommendations
will line up with what you picked.

If you're studying multiple things, choose your priority and use the
**course switcher** in the topbar to flip later.

### Your first game

The featured carousel is rotating through 12 flagships. Click any cabinet
card and the embedded player launches. First-run prompts will walk you
through the controls, and you'll start banking shards from the very first
correct answer.

**Pro tip:** play through the **Daily Challenge** before anything else.
Quick, free 60 shards, plus it counts toward your streak.

## 3. The Hub

The hub is the home page. It's organized as a stack of "rails" — each
rail is a different way of finding games.

### Topbar

Across the top: brand mark on the left, search box in the middle, course
filter + profile button on the right. Active buffs (Lucky Charm, Coin
Doubler) show as little pulsing chips so you know when your shards are
multiplying. Your shard count is permanently visible in JetBrains Mono —
that's the bronze number that ticks up every time you nail a question.

### Daily Challenge banner

Top of the page, right under the hero. Today's pick rotates daily from
the featured pool. Crush it once for **60 shards** (or **120** if you
have a Daily Double in your inventory). The streak indicator under the
banner shows your current run — don't let it die.

### Today's Plan

A 3-card row directly below the daily banner. The first card is
**recommended for you** based on what you've been playing recently (the
Recommender keeps a local model of your weakest topics). The other two
are seeded by your course choice. If a card is grayed out and locked,
it's gating on something — usually a previous game in a sequence.

### New Cabinet rail

Horizontal scroller of the freshest flagship games. New games get a
glowing **NEW** badge for two weeks after launch. If you see one you
haven't played, click it before the badge fades — there are usually
first-time-clear achievements attached.

### Library

Below the rails. The full searchable catalog: 199+ games. Filter by
**course**, **game type** (Brick Breaker, Tower Defense, Match-3, etc.),
**grade band**, or just type into the search bar at top. Hits highlight
in real time as you type. Cards show: title, course, game type, clue
count, and a tiny thumbnail preview.

**Saved searches:** the search box remembers your last query for the
session. Refreshing the page keeps the filter, but switching tabs or
restarting the browser clears it.

> **Try this!** Search for "snake" — you'll get Source Snake. Now search
> for "snake civics" — only the cross-curricular variants. Now search
> "history hunters" — you'll find both the original and the RPG sequel.

## 4. Profile Drawer

Click your avatar in the topbar to slide open the drawer from the right.
Five tabs across the top:

### Stats

Big-picture overview. Your total shards, lifetime games played, current
streak, longest streak, scholar-prompts answered correctly, and a tiny
sparkline of the last 14 days of activity. The streak number turns gold
when you hit a personal best.

### Streak Calendar

A 30-day grid of your daily activity. Filled cells = days you played
(at least one game). Outlined cells = days you missed but a Streak
Shield saved you. Bonus glyphs on weekend days you played (the
**Weekend Warrior** achievement).

### Achievements

All 65 achievements, grouped by tier (Bronze → Silver → Gold →
Legendary). Locked achievements show a lock icon and a partial progress
bar where the goal is countable (e.g. "47 / 100 plays" for the Centurion
achievement). Hidden achievements show only as a `?` until you unlock
them — no spoilers.

### Settings

Toggle motion (auto / on / off — respects `prefers-reduced-motion` from
your OS), sound, music volume, SFX volume, font family, color-blind
palette, and high-contrast mode. Changes apply instantly. The first
time you change a setting, you unlock the **Settings Tuned** Bronze
achievement.

### Shop

The Power-Up Shop. See section 5 for the full breakdown.

### Top Scores

Bottom of the drawer: your personal best in the top 3 games where you've
made the local leaderboard. Tap any entry to relaunch that game.

### Roster (multi-profile)

If multiple students share a Chromebook, click **Switch profile** at the
bottom of the drawer. See section 7 for how that works.

## 5. Power-Up Shop

Spend your shards on items that give you an edge. Seven items total. All
purchases are one-way — no refunds, no resells, but most items are cheap
enough to grind back quickly.

| Item | Cost | What it does |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Streak Shield** 🛡 | 200 shards | Saves your streak if you miss a day. Auto-consumed on the next gap. Stack as many as you want. |
| **Hint Token** 💡 | 50 shards | Eliminates one wrong answer choice on any quiz question. One per question. Best on hard scholar prompts. |
| **Time Boost** ⏱ | 75 shards | Adds 30 seconds to your timer in any timed mock exam. One use per boost. Critical on 28-question Regents simulators. |
| **Lucky Charm** ✨ | 350 shards | Doubles every shard you earn for 24 hours. Buffer chip pulses in the topbar while active. |
| **Fortune Refresh** 🔮 | 25 shards | Reroll today's daily fortune. One reroll per purchase. (The fortune is the flavor message in your daily banner — cosmetic but fun.) |
| **Daily Double** 📰 | 150 shards | Doubles your shard payout on the next Daily Challenge completion. Auto-consumed when you claim. |
| **Coin Doubler** 🪙 | 500 shards | Doubles every shard you earn for 4 hours. **Stacks with Lucky Charm** for a 4× effective multiplier. |

### Smart shopping order

1. Buy **Streak Shield** first. Always. One day off and your streak resets.
2. Buy **Daily Double** the night before a free day where you can grind.
3. Save up **3,500+ shards** before splurging on a **Lucky Charm** so
   you can earn during the 24-hour window.
4. **Coin Doubler + Lucky Charm together** = 4× shards for 4 hours.
   This is the highest yield combo in the arcade. Plan a 4-hour
   sprint around it.
5. **Hint Tokens + Time Boosts** are cheap; stockpile a few before
   any timed mock exam.

> **Try this!** Spend exactly 1,000 lifetime shards in the shop and
> you'll unlock the **Shop Patron** achievement.

## 6. Daily Challenge

Every 24 hours (local midnight), the arcade rotates a fresh **Daily
Challenge**: one specific game, picked from the featured pool, that
pays out **60 shards** when you claim. (120 with a Daily Double in
your inventory.) The shards stack with any active Lucky Charm or
Coin Doubler buffs.

### How to claim

- Open the daily challenge banner (top of the hub)
- Play the game, finish a run, score above the threshold
- The "Claim daily reward" button activates — click it
- Watch the coin shower

### Streak math

Your streak advances **every day you play any game**, not just the daily
challenge. But the daily challenge is the most reliable source of high-
quality shards per minute. Skip a day and:

- If you have a **Streak Shield**, it's auto-consumed and the streak
  survives. You'll see a toast: "Streak Shield used."
- If you don't, the streak resets to 1 the next time you play.

The **Daily Disciple** Gold achievement unlocks at **30 daily challenges
completed**. The **Centurion** Silver achievement unlocks at **100 total
plays** — daily-challenge plays count.

## 7. Multiple Profiles

Sharing a Chromebook with siblings or classmates? The arcade has a
built-in roster system. Each profile is a fully separate world — its
own shards, achievements, streak, settings, course, and saved sessions.

### How to add a new profile

1. Open the Profile Drawer (avatar in topbar)
2. Scroll to the bottom, click **Switch profile**
3. Click **+ New profile**
4. Pick a name, avatar, and course — same flow as first-time setup
5. The arcade switches to that profile immediately

### How to switch back

1. Profile Drawer → **Switch profile**
2. Click the profile in the list — it activates instantly
3. Topbar updates to reflect the new shards / streak / course

### How to rename or remove

- **Rename:** in the roster list, click the pencil icon next to a name
- **Remove:** click the X. (You can't remove the currently-active
  profile — switch first.)

### Tips

- Up to 24 profiles per device — way more than any classroom needs.
- Profiles don't compete on the same leaderboard; each profile has
  its own set of top-5 boards.
- The **first profile you create** becomes the legacy migration of any
  pre-roster data on that browser. You won't lose progress switching
  to the multi-profile system.

## 10. Tips & Tricks

### Keyboard shortcuts

- `Esc` — close any modal, dismiss the player, exit fullscreen
- `Enter` / `Space` — confirm answer or advance dialog
- `Tab` — cycle answer choices in scholar prompts
- `1` / `2` / `3` / `4` — directly select answer choice A/B/C/D in
  most quiz games
- `M` — mute/unmute (most flagship cabinets)
- `P` — pause (most action games)
- `R` — restart from checkpoint (where supported)

### Hidden features

- **Konami code** — `↑↑↓↓←→←→ B A` on the hub for **Code Master** + 200 shards + a confetti explosion. Works from any screen.
- **Brand-mark click** — tap "Mr. Mac's" in the topbar 7 times in 5 seconds for **Click Whisperer**.
- **Mascot click target** — the floating mascot has a click hitbox. Find it. (Hint: the eye blinks.)
- **Late-night play** — play between 11 PM and 4 AM for **Time Travel**.

### Cram Mode

A speed-review mode hidden behind the **Recommender** card on the hub.
It pulls your weakest topics from your wrong-answer queue and stitches
them into a 5-minute drill. Best 24 hours before an exam — don't try
it for the first time the morning of.

### Recommender

The "recommended for you" card on Today's Plan uses a local mastery
model. The more you play, the more the Recommender learns what you
need. After ~10 plays it gets pretty good. After ~50, it nails your
weakest unit-week combinations.

### The wrong-answer queue (Leitner box)

Every question you miss goes into a spaced-repetition queue. The
Recommender will surface those questions again 1 day, 3 days, 7 days,
14 days later (depending on how well you remember). Master a card 5
times in a row → it retires to the **Mastered Cards** archive.

### Shard farming routes

- **Daily Challenge** every day = 60 shards in 3 minutes
- **Lucky Charm + Coin Doubler stack** for 4 hours = 4× everything
- **Source Snake** scholar prompts = 20 shards each (+ 4× = 80)
- **Mock exam completion** = 200+ shards depending on score
- **Streak day-7** = bonus 75 shards

> **Try this!** Buy a Coin Doubler at 6:55 PM, then a Lucky Charm at
> 7:00 PM. Now you have 4 hours of 4× shards plus another 20 hours of
> 2× shards after the Coin Doubler runs out. Plan a long study session.

## 11. Troubleshooting

### "My profile disappeared"

99% of the time this is one of:

1. **You're in private/incognito mode.** Profile data is in
   `localStorage`; private mode wipes it on close. Use a normal window.
2. **You cleared browser data.** Settings → Privacy → "Clear data"
   nukes the arcade. There's no backup. Start fresh.
3. **You're on a different browser or device.** Profiles are per-browser-
   per-device. Chrome on your Chromebook is different from Safari on
   your phone.

### "The arcade looks broken / glitchy"

Hard refresh: `Ctrl + Shift + R` (Windows / Chromebook) or
`Cmd + Shift + R` (Mac). This reloads the latest CSS / JS.

If it persists:

1. Profile Drawer → Settings → toggle **Lite mode** on
2. Reload the page
3. If it works, your browser is too low-spec for full effects — leave
   Lite mode on

### "Reset my profile"

Profile Drawer → Settings → scroll to bottom → **Danger Zone** → **Reset
this profile**. Wipes shards, achievements, streaks, settings — but
**not the roster** (other profiles are untouched).

### "Reset everything"

Browser console (DevTools → F12 → Console tab) and run:

```js
localStorage.clear();
sessionStorage.clear();
location.reload();
```

This wipes the entire arcade including all roster profiles. You
can't undo this.

### "Music won't play"

iOS / Safari requires a user tap before audio works. Click anywhere
once and music will start. On Chromebooks, check that the OS volume
isn't muted (look at the speaker icon in the system tray).

### Browser compatibility

| Browser | Minimum | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Chrome / Edge | 90+ | Recommended. Full feature support. |
| Firefox | 88+ | Full support except some `:has()` styling on FF < 121. |
| Safari macOS | 14+ | Full support. iOS 16.0/16.1 has some accent-color quirks. |
| Safari iOS | 14+ | Use mobile portrait or landscape; full support. |

If you're on iOS 13 or older, parts of the arcade won't load — upgrade
your iOS or use Chrome on a different device.

## 12. Privacy

**TL;DR: Nothing leaves your device.**

The arcade is a static website hosted on GitHub Pages. There is no
backend, no API, no database, no user accounts, no logins, no email,
no PII collection.

Everything you do — your name, avatar, course, shards, streak,
achievements, mock exam scores, wrong-answer queue, leaderboard
position — is stored in your browser's `localStorage`. Only your
browser can read it. Only you can see it. We have no servers
collecting it.

The only network requests the arcade makes are:

- Loading the static site files (HTML, JS, CSS, WebP images, JSON)
  from GitHub Pages on first load
- An anonymous traffic counter that increments a global pageview
  number — no name, no profile, no answers transmitted
- That's it.

**No telemetry. No analytics on what questions you got wrong. No
shipping your scores to a teacher dashboard.** If you want to show
your teacher your progress, screenshot the Profile Drawer.

If you clear your browser data, your progress is gone — there is no
backup, no recovery, no "log in to restore." That's the trade-off for
zero data collection. Be intentional about which browser you use.
